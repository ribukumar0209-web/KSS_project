In Focus: Our Approach

Historical Flood Analysis ▼

Historical floodplain dynamics were reconstructed using multi decadal satellite archives to track shifts in surface water behavior. By analyzing metrics like absolute change, recurrence, and seasonality, the study identifies critical flood hotspots and distinguishes permanent channels from ephemeral water bodies, geospatial computing to ensure pixel level consistency across a 37 year observation period, providing a robust statistical baseline for how inundation footprints respond to long term climate variability.

River Space Demarcation ▼

River Space analysis was conducted by overlaying annual water masks from 1991 to 2025 to reconstruct active channel dynamics. By tracking migration pathways, braid belt expansion, and erosion zones, the study quantifies spatial variability in river mobility. This analysis is essential for identifying geomorphic instability and active floodways where shifting river positions directly threaten agricultural lands and critical flood defenses.

LULC Analysis ▼

Land use and land cover dynamics were evaluated using high-resolution satellite imagery to assess anthropogenic pressure on natural floodplains. By mapping transitions between water, vegetation, and built-up areas, the study quantifies the conversion of natural flood retention zones into impermeable surfaces or intensive agricultural plots.

Flood Hazard Prediction ▼

To predict flood hazards, a machine learning model was trained using decades of historical water data. The model identifies the primary drivers of inundation. This produces high resolution susceptibility maps that serve as diagnostic tools, pinpointing exactly where topographical depressions and weather patterns converge to create high risk hotspots.

Web GIS Integration ▼

A specialized Web GIS platform was engineered to synthesize complex research outputs into an interactive, open source interface. By optimizing high resolution rasters, the system transforms static data into a dynamic diagnostic tool. This centralized hub allows stakeholders to visualize the flood susceptibility, land use, and river space demarcation.

Policy Development & Cost-Benefit ▼

Translating scientific data into actionable policies, focusing on cost-benefit analysis and sustainable technology.
