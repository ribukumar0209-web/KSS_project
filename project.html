Towards Flood Resilient Cities :
A Pilot Study in the Lower Ghaghra-Rapti River Basin

Developing an integrated hydro-geomorphic framework using satellite data and machine learning to guide climate-resilient urban planning.

Engineering a Climate Resilient Future for the Ghaghra-Rapti Basin

This study bridges the gap between complex river science and safety by developing an integrated hydro geomorphic framework for the cities of Ayodhya, Gorakhpur, and Turtipar. By blending 30+ years of satellite observations with high resolution drone datasets and machine learning, we analyze the shifting memory of the river from channel mobility and sandbar evolution to human encroachment on floodplains.

Under the Kotak School of Sustainability (KSS), our multidisciplinary approach translates these scientific insights into actionable urban policies and nature based solutions, moving beyond disaster response toward a proactive, evidence based model of resilience for India’s most sensitive riverine environments.

Regional Focus Areas

Ayodhya

Ayodhya’s dynamic braided river system faces constant channel migration, requiring integrated strategies to protect vulnerable communities, livelihoods, and urban infrastructure.

Gorakhpur

Gorakhpur’s stable meandering Rapti system supports dense settlements facing seasonal flooding, requiring integrated drainage and sustainable urban resilience strategies.

Turtipar

Turtipar’s braided Ghaghra system faces migrating channels and erosion, requiring long-term geomorphic study to manage land loss and flood risks.

Methodology

1. Historical Flood Analysis

Historical floodplain dynamics were reconstructed using multi decadal satellite archives to track shifts in surface water behavior. By analyzing metrics like absolute change, recurrence, and seasonality, the study identifies critical flood hotspots and distinguishes permanent channels from ephemeral water bodies, geospatial computing to ensure pixel level consistency across a 37 year observation period, providing a robust statistical baseline for how inundation footprints respond to long term climate variability.

2. River Space Demarcation

River Space analysis was conducted by overlaying annual water masks from 1991 to 2025 to reconstruct active channel dynamics. By tracking migration pathways, braid belt expansion, and erosion zones, the study quantifies spatial variability in river mobility. This analysis is essential for identifying geomorphic instability and active floodways where shifting river positions directly threaten agricultural lands and critical flood defenses.

3. LULC Analysis

Land use and land cover dynamics were evaluated using high-resolution satellite imagery to assess anthropogenic pressure on natural floodplains. By mapping transitions between water, vegetation, and built-up areas, the study quantifies the conversion of natural flood retention zones into impermeable surfaces or intensive agricultural plots.

4. Flood Hazard Prediction

To predict flood hazards, a machine learning model was trained using decades of historical water data. The model identifies the primary drivers of inundation. This produces high resolution susceptibility maps that serve as diagnostic tools, pinpointing exactly where topographical depressions and weather patterns converge to create high risk hotspots.

5. Web GIS Integration and Visualization Platform

A specialized Web GIS platform was engineered to synthesize complex research outputs into an interactive, open source interface. By optimizing high resolution rasters, the system transforms static data into a dynamic diagnostic tool. This centralized hub allows stakeholders to visualize the intersections of flood susceptibility, land use, and river migration.

6. Development of regulatory policies and cost-benefit analysis

Translating scientific data into actionable policies, focusing on cost-benefit analysis and sustainable tech.
